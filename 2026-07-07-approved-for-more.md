# Approved for More ≠ Afford More

- **Type:** Reenactment (2 parts)
- **Status:** Scripted
- **Date approved:** 2026-07-07
- **CTA:** None
- **Image asset:** None

*Jordan plays all roles: **BORROWER** (feeling lowballed, then humbled) and **JORDAN** (calm, dry LO). Grounded tone with real numbers. The "other lender" is referenced, not a character. Each part runs 60–90 seconds.*

**Numbers used:** Jordan pre-approved ~$350k (≈ $2,900/mo, the borrower's stated comfort). Another lender approved $500k (≈ $3,900/mo — ~$1,000 more per month).

---

## PART 1 — "You lowballed me!"

**BORROWER:** Jordan, we need to talk. I feel like you don't believe in me.

**JORDAN:** …What happened.

**BORROWER:** I called another lender. Just to check. They approved me for five hundred thousand.

**JORDAN:** Okay—

**BORROWER:** You said three-fifty! That's a hundred and fifty grand less! You don't think I can handle it?

**JORDAN:** It's not about handling it—

**BORROWER:** *They* believe in me, Jordan.

**JORDAN:** Can I ask you one question?

**BORROWER:** …Fine.

**JORDAN:** When they said five hundred thousand — did they tell you the monthly payment?

**BORROWER:** …The payment?

**JORDAN:** The payment.

**BORROWER:** They said the *number.* Five hundred. It was very exciting.

**JORDAN:** Right. But what would you actually pay every month?

**BORROWER:** …I assumed it was, like, normal.

**JORDAN:** Grab a calculator. Let's do the part they skipped.

**TEXT OVERLAY (end card):** *Part 2 — the number they didn't tell you.*

---

## PART 2 — "The number they didn't tell you"

**JORDAN:** Okay. When you sat with me — what did you say felt comfortable? Monthly.

**BORROWER:** Like twenty-nine hundred. That's what fits my life.

**JORDAN:** Right. That's why I said three-fifty. That's a payment around twenty-nine hundred a month.

**BORROWER:** Okay…

**JORDAN:** Now that shiny five-hundred-thousand approval? That payment's about thirty-nine hundred a month.

**BORROWER:** …Thirty-nine.

**JORDAN:** A thousand dollars more. Every month. For thirty years.

**BORROWER:** *(quiet)* That's… a lot of DoorDash.

**JORDAN:** It's a car payment. On top of your house. Forever.

**BORROWER:** So they didn't believe in me. They just approved me for the max I qualify for *on paper.*

**JORDAN:** Getting approved and being able to *afford* it — not the same thing. The bank doesn't have to make that payment. You do.

**BORROWER:** …Three-fifty's actually kind of perfect.

**JORDAN:** I know. I do this for a living.

**BORROWER:** Okay, don't get cocky.

---

## PEACE
- **Problem:** Thinking a bigger approval means a better lender (or a better deal).
- **Empathy:** A higher number feels like validation; a lower one feels like a lowball.
- **Answer:** "Approved" is the max you qualify for on paper — not what's comfortable; the payment is what matters.
- **Change:** Buy the monthly payment that fits your life, not the ceiling the bank hands you.
- **End Result:** A home that doesn't leave you house-poor.
