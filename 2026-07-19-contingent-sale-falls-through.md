# Contingent on My House Selling

- **Type:** Reenactment (2 parts)
- **Status:** Scripted
- **Date approved:** 2026-07-19
- **CTA:** None
- **Image asset:** None
- **Need:** Certainty + Contribution

*Jordan plays both roles: **DANIELLE** (panicked buyer) and **JORDAN** (calm, solution-focused LO). Each part runs 60–90 seconds.*

---

## PART 1 — "We have a huge problem"

**DANIELLE:** *(calling, panicked)* Jordan. Jordan, hi, sorry to call so late — we have a huge problem.

**JORDAN:** Danielle, hey, breathe. What's going on?

**DANIELLE:** Our buyer just backed out. The people buying our house — their financing fell through, like, an hour ago. And we close on the new house in four days.

**JORDAN:** Okay. Four days from today?

**DANIELLE:** Four days! And our whole contract on the new house was contingent on our house selling. Doesn't that mean we lose the new house? Do we lose our earnest money? What happens now?

**JORDAN:** Okay, first — nobody's losing the house tonight. I need you to slow down for one second. Is your old house still actively listed, or was it under contract and off the market?

**DANIELLE:** It was under contract, so it's been off the market for like five weeks.

**JORDAN:** Okay. And how much equity do you have in that house, ballpark?

**DANIELLE:** I don't know, maybe close to ninety thousand?

**JORDAN:** Alright, that actually matters a lot right now. I have a few ways we can look at this, but I need you to not panic-call your realtor and tell them to cancel anything yet. Let me look at your file tonight and call you first thing in the morning. Can you do that for me?

**DANIELLE:** Okay. Okay, yes. I just — I really don't want to lose this house.

**JORDAN:** I hear you. We're not there yet. Let me work the problem first.

**TEXT OVERLAY (end card):** *Part 2 — the plan.*

---

## PART 2 — "There's almost always more than one way through it"

**JORDAN:** Danielle, hey, it's Jordan. I looked at everything last night — I think we have a real path here.

**DANIELLE:** Please. I've barely slept.

**JORDAN:** Okay, so — first thing, on your new house closing, we requested a five-day extension from the seller this morning, and they agreed to it, since you've been such a clean buyer through this whole process.

**DANIELLE:** Oh my god. Okay.

**JORDAN:** That buys us breathing room. Now, for the down payment — since your old house isn't sold yet, we're going to use a short-term bridge loan against the equity you have in it. That covers your down payment on the new house without needing your old house to close first.

**DANIELLE:** Wait, that's allowed?

**JORDAN:** It is, and it's more common than people think, especially when a sale falls through this late. Meanwhile, your realtor's already re-listing the old house today, and honestly, with the market where it is, I'd expect a new buyer within a couple weeks.

**DANIELLE:** So we still close on the new house.

**JORDAN:** You still close on the new house. On time, or pretty close to it. This is exactly why we don't panic and cancel everything the second something goes wrong. There's almost always more than one way through it.

**DANIELLE:** Jordan, I could cry right now.

**JORDAN:** Save it for the closing table. Let's get you moved in.

---

## PEACE
- **Problem:** A buyer's home sale falls through days before their own closing on a contingent contract.
- **Empathy:** The instinct is to panic and assume the deal is dead.
- **Answer:** A closing extension plus a short-term bridge loan against existing equity solves the timing gap.
- **Change:** Don't cancel anything the moment something breaks — work the problem first.
- **End Result:** The buyer closes on time, and keeps the home.
