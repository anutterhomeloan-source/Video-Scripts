## Hook (Misconception Flip)
Everybody thinks you need two years of tax returns to buy an investment property. …Nope. There's a loan that doesn't care what *you* make at all.

## Branding
If you don't know me, I'm Jordan Nutter, and I help make mortgages make sense.

## Problem
So here's the deal for real estate investors. Normally, to buy a rental, the bank digs through your tax returns. Your income. Your debt. And if you already own a few properties? Your debt-to-income maxes out fast. Deal dead.

## Empathy
And it's frustrating. You *know* the property makes money. But on paper, you look overextended.

## Answer
That's what a DSCR loan is for. DSCR just means — does the rent cover the payment? That's it. We don't use your income. We don't use your tax returns. We look at the property.

VISUAL: "DSCR = does the rent cover the payment?" card (dscr-rent-covers-card.png)

## Change (numbers)
Let me show you. Say the rent is $2,500 a month. And the payment — with taxes and insurance — is $2,100. The rent covers it, with room to spare. That property qualifies. On its own. Your personal income never even comes up.

## End Result
So if you've been on the sidelines because your tax returns don't "look good enough" — this might be the thing that gets you back in the game.
