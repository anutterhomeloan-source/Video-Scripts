## HOOK
If you're waiting for rates to drop before you buy a house — I need 60 seconds, because this is probably costing you more than you think.

## BRANDING
If we haven't met — I'm Jordan Nutter, and I help make mortgages make sense.

## PROBLEM + STORY
I had a couple last year who were so close to buying. Found a house they loved, around $400,000. But they go, "Jordan, we're gonna wait — rates are too high, we'll buy when they come down." Totally reasonable, right? So they waited.

## EMPATHY
And listen — I get the instinct. Nobody wants to lock in a payment that feels high. Waiting feels like the safe, smart move. You're not wrong for thinking that.

**VISUAL:** Text overlay — "Waiting feels safe. Here's the catch."

## ANSWER
But here's what actually happens when rates drop. Everybody who was sitting on the sidelines — they jump back in at the same time. Demand spikes. Suddenly you're up against eight other offers, prices get bid up, and that house you loved? It costs more now.

So that same couple — they came back six months later. Rates had dipped a little, like they hoped. But that $400,000 house? It was $430,000, and they were in a bidding war for it.

**VISUAL:** 2-line example — "Waited for: rate ↓ 1% · Reality: price ↑ $30K + 8 competing offers"

## CHANGE
Here's the part most people miss: your rate isn't permanent. If rates drop later, you can refinance — that's a phone call. But the price you pay for the house? That's locked in forever. You don't get to renegotiate it.

## END RESULT + CTA
So the buyers who win aren't the ones who timed it perfectly — they're the ones who bought the right house when they found it, and adjusted the rate later. If you're stuck on whether to wait or jump now, don't guess — schedule a call with me and let's actually run your numbers. Link's in my bio.
