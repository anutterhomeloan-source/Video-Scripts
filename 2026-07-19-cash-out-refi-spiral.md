# The Cash-Out Refi Debt Spiral

- **Type:** Reenactment (2 parts)
- **Status:** Scripted
- **Date approved:** 2026-07-19
- **CTA:** None
- **Image asset:** None
- **Need:** Certainty (avoid pain) + Variety (counterintuitive debt reframe)

*Jordan plays both roles: **RYAN** (borrower, a year out from a cash-out refi) and **JORDAN** (calm, direct LO). Each part runs 60–90 seconds.*

---

## PART 1 — "It was a game changer"

**RYAN:** Jordan! Hey, it's been a minute.

**JORDAN:** Ryan! Good to see you. What's going on, you looking to refi again already?

**RYAN:** No, no — actually, I just wanted to say thank you. That cash-out refi we did last year? Total game changer. I paid off like eighteen grand in credit cards. Zeroed everything out. Felt amazing.

**JORDAN:** That's great, Ryan, seriously. How's it feel now, a year later?

**RYAN:** Honestly? Really good. I mean, my mortgage payment went up a little, but that's fine, because I don't have those card payments hanging over me anymore.

**JORDAN:** Okay. And the cards — you still have zero balances on all of them?

**RYAN:** *(pause)* Well — I mean, I still have the cards. I didn't close them or anything.

**JORDAN:** Right, you shouldn't close them. But are they still at zero?

**RYAN:** ...Why do you ask it like that.

**JORDAN:** Because I ask everybody that question a year after a debt consolidation. Not because I don't trust you — because I've seen this movie before. So. Where are the balances at now, actually?

**RYAN:** I mean... some of them have crept back up. Not like before, but — yeah. There's some balance there again.

**JORDAN:** Okay. Be honest with me for a second, Ryan.

**RYAN:** *(sighs)* Yeah. Okay.

**TEXT OVERLAY (end card):** *Part 2 — the real number.*

---

## PART 2 — "The mortgage was never the problem"

**RYAN:** Fine. They're not just "some balance." They're pretty much maxed out again. Same as before we did the refi.

**JORDAN:** Okay. I appreciate you being straight with me. How much are we talking?

**RYAN:** Probably... sixteen, seventeen thousand. Almost exactly what we paid off last year.

**JORDAN:** Right. So here's what actually happened, Ryan — and I say this to a lot of people, not just you. The refi paid off the debt. It didn't fix why the debt was there in the first place. That balance didn't come from nowhere. It came from a spending pattern that never actually changed.

**RYAN:** So what, I just did all that for nothing?

**JORDAN:** Not for nothing — you lowered your rate, and you got rid of that debt for a year. That's real. But if we do another cash-out right now to clean this up again, and nothing else changes, we'll be sitting here having this same conversation in twelve months. Except with less equity to work with next time.

**RYAN:** That's... kind of a gut punch, not gonna lie.

**JORDAN:** I know. But I'd rather tell you that now than refi you straight into a hole. Before we touch the loan again, I want to know what changed after last year's payoff. Whatever that answer is — that's the actual thing we need to fix. The mortgage was never the problem.

**RYAN:** Yeah. Okay. That's fair.

**JORDAN:** That's the conversation. Let's have it before we touch anything else.

---

## PEACE
- **Problem:** A cash-out refi paid off debt, but a year later the same cards are maxed out again.
- **Empathy:** It felt like a real win at the time — because it was, temporarily.
- **Answer:** A refi clears the balance, not the habit that created it.
- **Change:** Identify what actually changed (or didn't) since the payoff before touching the loan again.
- **End Result:** Fix the real problem instead of refinancing into a deeper hole.
