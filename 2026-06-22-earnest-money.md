## Hook (Misconception Flip)
Earnest money is just a fee you hand over and never see again. …And that's one of the most expensive misconceptions in home buying. Because last month I had a buyer text me in a full panic — "Jordan, if this deal falls apart, do I just *lose* my eight grand?" She was *this close* to walking away from a house she loved over money that was never gone. I'm not talking about earnest money you throw away by breaking the rules — I'm talking about money that's yours, sitting there working *for* you.

## Branding
If you don't know me, I'm Jordan Nutter, and I help make mortgages make sense.

## Answer
Earnest money is a good-faith deposit — it tells the seller you're serious. But it's *your* money. It goes straight toward your down payment and closing costs. And it's protected by your contingencies — financing, inspection, appraisal.

VISUAL: Text overlay — "Earnest money = a fee you lose ❌" then crossed out → "Earnest money = YOUR money ✅"

## Change
So two things. One — stop thinking of it as a fee you'll never see. Two — don't blindly waive the contingencies that protect it just to win a bid. Those are the seatbelts on your deposit.

VISUAL: Checklist overlay — "3 things that protect your deposit: ✅ Financing ✅ Inspection ✅ Appraisal"

## End Result
When you actually understand it, you stop white-knuckling your offer. Your money's not gone — it's applied to *your* purchase. That's the whole point.
