# 20% Down to Skip PMI

- **Type:** Reenactment (2 parts)
- **Status:** Posted
- **Date approved:** 2026-06-22
- **CTA:** None
- **Image asset:** None

*Jordan plays all roles: **BORROWER** (solo first-timer, proud then nervous) and **JORDAN** (calm, dry LO). Comedic-panic tone. Each part runs 60–90 seconds.*

---

## PART 1 — "I'm wiping out my savings"

**BORROWER:** *(proud)* Jordan, I figured it out. Full 20% down. Every penny I've got. No PMI. Beat that.

**JORDAN:** Okay… when you say *every* penny—

**BORROWER:** I mean *every* penny. Checking, savings, the little emergency fund, my "do-not-touch" account—

**JORDAN:** You're touching the do-not-touch account.

**BORROWER:** For 20%? Absolutely. I am NOT paying that PMI thing. I read about it. It's a scam.

**JORDAN:** It's not a scam, but okay. So after all of that goes down — how much do you have left? In the bank. The day *after* closing.

**BORROWER:** …left over?

**JORDAN:** Left over.

**BORROWER:** *(long pause)* …Define "left over."

**JORDAN:** Money. That exists. After. For things.

**BORROWER:** …I mean. The house *is* the thing.

**JORDAN:** Mhm. And what happens when the water heater goes out in week two?

**BORROWER:** Why would the water heater go out?? It's MY house now, it wouldn't do that to me.

**JORDAN:** Oh, it would absolutely do that to you. New houses *love* to do that to you.

**BORROWER:** *(nervous)* …How much is a water heater.

**JORDAN:** Sit down. Let's talk about this PMI thing you hate so much.

**TEXT OVERLAY (end card):** *Part 2: what PMI actually costs.*

---

## PART 2 — "What PMI actually costs"

**JORDAN:** Okay. PMI. You think it's a scam. Why?

**BORROWER:** Because it protects the *lender*, not me! Why am I paying for THEIR insurance?

**JORDAN:** Fair. It does protect the lender. But here's the part nobody tells you — it's *temporary*. Once you've got about 20% equity, it drops off. You're not paying it for 30 years.

**BORROWER:** …it goes away?

**JORDAN:** It goes away. And on your loan, we're talking maybe a hundred-ish bucks a month — to keep what, fifteen, twenty grand in the bank?

**BORROWER:** *(quietly doing math)* …So I could put less down, keep my emergency fund, and just… pay a little PMI for a bit.

**JORDAN:** And the day the water heater explodes, you don't have to sell a kidney.

**BORROWER:** …I do like both my kidneys.

**JORDAN:** Keep your kidneys. Keep your cushion. We'll get you in with a down payment that doesn't leave you broke and praying nothing breaks.

**BORROWER:** *(relieved)* Okay. …So PMI's not a scam.

**JORDAN:** It's a tool. Same as your emergency fund — which you're keeping.

**BORROWER:** Which I'm keeping.

**JORDAN:** Which you're keeping.

---

## PEACE
- **Problem:** Draining all savings to hit 20% and avoid PMI.
- **Empathy:** PMI has a bad rep; nobody explains it, so you assume the worst.
- **Answer:** PMI is temporary (drops at ~20–22% equity) and often small; keeping a cash cushion matters more.
- **Change:** Put down an amount that leaves you with reserves; treat PMI as a tool, not a penalty.
- **End Result:** You buy without going broke — protected if something breaks.

---

## CAPTIONS
- **Part 1:** Draining your whole savings to skip PMI might be the most expensive "smart" move there is. 👇
- **Part 2:** PMI isn't a scam — it's temporary, and it might be the cheapest way to keep your emergency fund. Keep your kidneys. 😅
