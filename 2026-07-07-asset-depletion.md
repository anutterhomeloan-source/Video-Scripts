## Story / Problem
I had a client. Retired. Worked her whole life, saved beautifully. Over a million in savings and investments. But her monthly income on paper? Small. Just what she pulled from Social Security. So the bank said no. Not enough monthly income.

## Empathy
And she was stunned. She goes, "Jordan, I have more in the bank than the house even costs." I know. It's backwards.

## Answer
Here's the fix. It's called an asset-based loan — or asset depletion. Instead of looking at your paycheck, we take your assets. Your savings, your investments. And we turn them into qualifying income.

## Change
So all that money she saved? It finally counted for something. We used her nest egg to qualify her — without her draining it or going back to work.

## End Result
If you're asset-rich but income-light — retired, or living off your investments — you are not stuck. There's a whole loan built for exactly this.
