## Hook (Problem)
If you're in the middle of buying a house — please, do not accept a raise without calling me first. I know how that sounds. Stay with me.

## Branding
I'm Jordan Nutter, and I help make mortgages make sense.

## Empathy + Story
I had a buyer doing *everything* right. She was using a down payment assistance program to cover most of her down payment — money she didn't have to come up with herself. Then, mid-application, she got a raise. Amazing news, right? Except that raise pushed her *over the income limit* for the program. And just like that, the help she was counting on… gone.

VISUAL: Before/after card — "Under income limit ✅ → Raise → Over limit ❌ (lost the DPA)"

## Answer
Here's what nobody tells you: a lot of the best programs — down payment assistance, first-time buyer programs — have *income limits*. Which means more income can actually *disqualify* you. It's the one time in life a raise can work against you.

## Change
So before you accept a raise, a big bonus, a new job, or switch to 1099 — anything that moves your income — loop in your lender first. A lot of times we can just *time* it differently so you keep the program AND the raise.

VISUAL: Checklist overlay — "Tell your lender BEFORE: 💵 a raise · 🎉 a bonus · 🧳 a new job · 🧾 going 1099"

## End Result
One quick heads-up and she'd have kept thousands in assistance. Don't let good news blow up your loan.

## CTA
If anything about your income is about to change while you're buying, let's get on a quick call *first* — link's in my bio. Two minutes now saves you a scramble later.
