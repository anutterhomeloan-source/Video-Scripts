## Hook (Flip)
There's one number that tells you whether you're "middle class" in America.

That's how these headlines get written.

And that one number means something completely different depending on your zip code — especially when it comes to what house it actually buys you.

I'm not talking about comparing yourself to a national average.

I'm talking about what your income actually qualifies you for, where you live.

## Branding
If you don't know who I am, my name is Jordan Nutter, and I help make mortgages make sense.

VISUAL: headline overlay of the middle-class income study, sourced "SmartAsset 2026 study, via Yahoo Finance" (middle-class-income-card.png)

## Problem
A new study on "middle class income" is going around right now.

And on paper, it looks simple. One range, one number.

## Empathy
But that number gets confusing fast, because the same income can feel completely different depending on where you live.

## Answer
In Mississippi, middle class runs from about $39,000 to $118,000.

In states like Massachusetts, California, or New Jersey, that range runs all the way past $200,000.

VISUAL: state-by-state bar comparison (Mississippi vs. Massachusetts/California range)

## Change
So stop comparing your income to a national headline.

Compare it to what homes actually cost, and what you actually qualify for, in your market.

## End Result
That's the number that matters. Not the one in the headline.
