## Hook (Flip)
You have to sell your current house before you can buy the next one.

That's just how it works, right?

And believing that is exactly what makes people miss out on the house they actually wanted.
Or panic and sell the one they're in for less than it's worth, just to make the timing work.

I'm not talking about some risky workaround here.

I'm talking about a program that gives you a real, written backup offer on your old house — before you even list it.

## Branding
If you don't know who I am, my name is Jordan Nutter, and I help make mortgages make sense.

## Problem
Here's what usually happens.

You find the house you want.

But you're told you can't make a real offer, because your current house hasn't sold yet.

So either you lose that house.

Or you rush your sale and leave money on the table.

## Empathy
And that's genuinely how it's worked for most people, for a long time.

Nobody's told you it's changed.

VISUAL: text overlay "You don't have to sell first"

## Answer
There's a bridge loan program I work with called Knock.

It lets you use the equity that's tied up in your current home — before you sell it.

That money can go toward your down payment.
Toward moving costs.
Even toward getting your old house ready to sell.

## Change
And here's the part that actually solves the timing problem.

It comes with a backup offer, in writing, to buy your old house if it hasn't sold in six months.

So you're never stuck paying two mortgages, hoping your old house sells in time.

VISUAL: before/after — "Old way: sell first, then scramble to buy" vs. "This way: buy first, backup offer on the old house" (knock-buy-before-sell-card.png)

## End Result
Which means you get to buy the home you actually want.

On your timeline.

Without torching the value of the one you're leaving.

If this sounds like your situation, schedule a call with me and let's see if it's a fit.
