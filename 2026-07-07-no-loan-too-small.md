*Based on a real client: buying a home from family, small loan (~$80K), brushed off by a large national online lender because it wasn't "worth their time." Jordan took it and closed it. (Do NOT name the lender on camera.)*

---

## Hook (Stance)
I'll never forget the client one of those giant online lenders brushed off — because her loan was "too small" to bother with. It still makes me a little mad.

## Branding
If you don't know me, I'm Jordan Nutter, and I help make mortgages make sense.

## Story / Problem
She was buying a home from family. Smaller loan — around eighty thousand dollars. And to one of those big national lenders? That's not worth their time. Too small. So they brushed her off. Made her feel like a bother. Like her home mattered less because the number was smaller.

## Stance / Answer
And here's what I think about that. No loan is too small. No client is too small. If you're buying a home — whether it's eighty thousand or eight hundred thousand — you deserve someone who actually shows up for you.

VISUAL: "No loan is too small. No client is too small." bold text card

## Change
So we took it. We treated her file like it was our biggest deal of the year. Because to *her* — it was the biggest deal of her life. And we got her that home.

## End Result
A big lender treating you like a number isn't the end of your story. It just means you were talking to the wrong people.

## CTA
If you've ever been made to feel like your loan wasn't "worth it" — come talk to me. Schedule a call, link in my bio. I promise you're worth the time.
