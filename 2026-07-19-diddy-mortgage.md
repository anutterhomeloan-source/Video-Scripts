## Problem
Diddy just sold his Star Island mansion for $55 million.

The buyer could have paid every dollar of that in cash.

Instead, they took out an $18.5 million mortgage.

## Empathy
And I get why that sounds backwards.
Most of us grew up being told debt is something you avoid. Period.

## Answer
But at that level, a mortgage isn't a sign you couldn't afford the house.

It's a financial tool.

Keep your cash liquid. Keep it invested. Let the math work in your favor if that investment outperforms the interest rate.

VISUAL: 2-line comparison — "Pay cash: house paid off, cash gone" vs. "Finance: house paid off over time, cash still working for you"

## Change
And that math question isn't just for a $55 million house.

It's the same question at $350,000.

Could that cash do more for you somewhere else than it saves you in interest?

## End Result
Once you start asking that question, a mortgage stops feeling like a burden.

It starts looking like a strategy.
