## Hook (Flip)
Property taxes are something you deal with once a year.
A bill shows up, you pay it, you move on.

That's what most people think.

And that's the part that catches homeowners off guard — because your property tax isn't separate from your mortgage payment.
It's baked into it. Every single month.

I'm not talking about your tax bill going up.

I'm talking about your mortgage payment going up — without your rate ever changing.

## Problem
Right now, Florida has Amendment 3 headed to voters this November.
It would raise the homestead exemption to $250,000, and cap how much non-homestead property values can jump each year.

## Empathy
And if you're not in Florida — stick with me, because a lot of states are running versions of this same fight right now.

## Answer
Here's the part nobody explains.

Most of us don't pay property tax in one lump sum.
Your lender collects a piece of it every month, along with your insurance, and holds it in something called an escrow account.

VISUAL: simple 2-line chart — "monthly payment = principal + interest + taxes + insurance," taxes/insurance portion highlighted

## Change
So when your assessed value changes — up or down — your monthly payment can change with it.
Nothing to do with your interest rate at all.

If you're watching a ballot measure like this one, ask your lender for an escrow analysis, so you actually know where you stand.

## End Result
That way, a headline about property taxes doesn't turn into a surprise on your mortgage statement six months later.
