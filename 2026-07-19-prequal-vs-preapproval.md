## Hook (Flip)
A pre-qualification letter means you're ready to make an offer.

That's what everyone assumes.

And that's exactly why some offers get passed over — before the seller even finishes reading them.

I'm not talking about having no paperwork at all.

I'm talking about having the wrong kind of paperwork... and not knowing it.

## Branding
If you don't know who I am, my name is Jordan Nutter, and I help make mortgages make sense.

## Problem
So here's the thing.
Pre-qualified and pre-approved sound like the same word with an extra step.

They are not the same thing.

## Empathy
And honestly — why would you know that?
Nobody sits you down and explains the difference.
The words are basically designed to confuse you.

VISUAL: side-by-side "Pre-Qual vs. Pre-Approval" table overlay

## Answer
Pre-qualification is a guess.
You tell someone your income, your credit, roughly what you think it is.
Nobody verifies anything.

Pre-approval is different.
Your income is verified.
Your credit is actually pulled.
Your assets are checked.
An underwriter has looked at it — not just a loan officer typing numbers into a screen.

VISUAL: text overlay quoting the myth — "it's basically the same thing" — with a red X

## Change
So before you go look at a single house, ask your lender one direct question.

"Is this verified, or is this estimated?"

That one sentence tells you exactly what you're walking in with.

## End Result
Because in a competitive market, sellers know the difference too.

An offer backed by a real pre-approval gets taken seriously.

An offer backed by a guess... doesn't.
