## Hook (Flip)
Attaching your full pre-approval letter to every offer makes you look like a strong buyer.

Wrong.

That habit is quietly costing my buyers here in Atlanta thousands, every time they write one.

Because every buyer I work with gets a brand new letter, for every offer.

I'm not talking about hiding what you're approved for.

I'm talking about not handing the seller your ceiling before they've even read your price.

## Branding
If you don't know who I am, my name is Jordan Nutter, and I help make mortgages make sense.

## Answer
Here's why. The listing agent reads your pre-approval number before they read your price.

Say you're approved for $550,000, offering $475.

Letter says $550? You just told them your ceiling. They counter you up.

Big banks send one letter, for your max, done.

## Change
I write a new one, every offer. Offering $475? Letter says $475.

## End Result
Takes three minutes. Cheapest leverage in this whole process.

Ask your lender: will you rewrite my letter, every offer?

Yes? You've got somebody in your corner.

Same generic letter every time? Find a new lender.
