## Hook (Flip — puzzle)
Two houses. Same exact price, same loan, same rate. So the same monthly payment, right? …Wrong — and that wrong answer is exactly how people end up house-poor. Because one of them sits in a county with way higher property taxes, and those taxes ride *inside* your monthly payment. I'm not talking about the loan — I'm talking about the taxes and insurance bundled in right next to it.

## Answer
Here's the thing nobody explains until closing day: your monthly payment isn't just the loan. It's PITI — Principal, Interest, Taxes, and Insurance. Your property taxes and homeowners insurance get bundled right into that monthly number through something called escrow. And on some homes? That's *hundreds* of dollars a month on top of the loan itself.

VISUAL: PITI breakdown — pie or 4-bar: "Principal · Interest · TAXES · Insurance" with Taxes highlighted

## Change
So when you're shopping, don't just ask "what's my principal and interest?" Ask for the *full* payment — taxes and insurance included. Two identical-priced houses in two different counties can have totally different monthly payments because of this.

## End Result
That's how you avoid the closing-table gut-punch where the payment's $400 higher than the number stuck in your head. No surprises — just your real number.
