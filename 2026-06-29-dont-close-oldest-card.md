# Don't Close Your Oldest Credit Card

## Hook (Misconception Flip)
Closing a credit card you never use is just good housekeeping. …It's also one of the fastest ways to tank your score right before you buy.

## Branding
If you don't know me — I'm Jordan Nutter, and I help make mortgages make sense.

## Story
I had a client. Doing everything right. She wanted to clean up her credit before applying. So she closed her oldest card. The one from college she never used. Felt responsible. Her score dropped almost 40 points. Right before we pulled it.

VISUAL: "Closed oldest card → score −40?" text card (close-card-score-drop.png)

## Empathy
And I get it. An old card you don't use feels like clutter. Closing it feels like the smart move.

## Answer
But two things happen when you close it. One — you lose that card's history. Older accounts make your score stronger. Two — you lose its credit limit. So your other balances suddenly look bigger. Both drag your score down.

## Change
So before you buy? Don't close anything. Keep the old card open. Put one small thing on it. A streaming subscription. Set it to auto-pay. Now it's alive and you never think about it.

VISUAL: "Keep it open + 1 small auto-charge" tip card

## End Result
That little card might be the thing holding your score up. My client? We got hers back. But it cost her a few weeks she didn't have to lose.

## CTA
If you're not sure what to touch and what to leave alone before you buy — don't guess. Schedule a call with me first. Link's in my bio. Let's clean it up the right way.
